## Staff Won't Answer Basic Questions

Three weeks after emergency responders were dispatched to Senator Mitch McConnell's Washington, D.C., residence, his office is still refusing to answer straightforward questions about his condition. [The Daily Beast reported](https://www.thedailybeast.com/mitch-mcconnells-goons-david-popp-and-robert-steurer-cornered-on-truth-about-his-health/) that spokesperson Robert Steurer declined to respond when asked whether McConnell remains in a hospital, when he was discharged, whether staff have spoken with him since he was found unconscious, or what diagnosis he has received.

When pressed for additional information, Steurer referred reporters to the same statement his office released more than a week earlier.

## Timeline Since June 14

Publicly released EMS records indicate that the 84-year-old Kentucky Republican may have suffered a heart attack on June 14, when he was found unconscious at home and [administered CPR](https://www.thedailybeast.com/mitch-mcconell-84-secretly-found-unconscious-and-given-cpr/). His staff acknowledged that same day that he had been hospitalized.

It was not until June 22 that his office offered any further update. Spokesperson David Popp told [Politico](https://www.politico.com/live-updates/2026/06/22/congress/mcconnell-still-out-00971071) that McConnell was *"still working closely with staff on Senate business and Kentucky matters as he continues his recovery"* but would not be voting that week. No details about his medical situation were included.

## Who Is Handling the Silence

Steurer reports to McConnell's chief of staff, Terry Carmack, whose Senate salary rose to roughly $226,850 in fiscal year 2026. Carmack, 63, began working for the newly elected McConnell [in 1985](https://www.politico.com/magazine/story/2015/08/12-steps-to-a-capitol-hill-job-121012/) and has remained at his side for decades, though he rarely appears in public.

## Kentucky Succession Context

McConnell's health scare comes two years after Kentucky Republicans changed how a vacant Senate seat would be filled. Previously, Democratic Gov. Andy Beshear would have appointed a replacement to serve until the next election. In April 2024, the General Assembly passed legislation — [House Bill 622](https://apps.legislature.ky.gov/record/26rs/hb622.html) — requiring a special election instead, a shift Beshear criticized as a reversal of precedent dating to 1942.

Kentucky Republicans moved to alter the system amid several health scares McConnell has experienced during his tenure. In February 2026, he was hospitalized for about eight days after flu-like symptoms. In October, he fell at the Capitol; he had previously injured his wrist in December 2024 and suffered a concussion after a hotel fall in 2023.

McConnell, who is set to retire at the end of the year, was the only Republican to oppose Tulsi Gabbard's confirmation as national intelligence director and Robert F. Kennedy Jr.'s appointment as health secretary. He also joined Sens. Lisa Murkowski and Susan Collins in voting against Pete Hegseth's confirmation as defense secretary.

## How This Fits the Broader Story

The Daily Beast report adds to a pattern of strict silence from McConnell's team that we have tracked since the [June 14 emergency](/blog/mitch-mcconnell-found-unconscious/) and the [911 call transcript](/blog/911-call/) that surfaced afterward. Dispatch audio referenced CPR and a possible cardiac arrest, but his office has never confirmed those details.
