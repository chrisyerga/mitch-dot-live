## Online Whispers But Not Much Else
Rumors will grow to fill an information vacuum, and the rumor mill has been spreading online regarding
Senator Mitch McConnell. The Senator was hospitalized over two weeks ago on June 14th, his second in 2026. His staff issued a terse statement that he was "recovering" would not be voting the week of June 22, but after that week passed there has been no update.

Online speculation has been rampant with reports by widely-followed independent journalists like [Aaron Parnas](https://www.tiktok.com/@aaronparnas1), [The David Packman Show](https://www.facebook.com/reel/2489465774885586) and [Epistemic](https://www.tiktok.com/@epistemiccrisis) have been reporting on the rumors. No credible sources or new information has become available, so there's not much to report on aside from the rumors themselves.

## Death Certificate & False Motivations

One reason given for why news of the Senator's demise *might* be withheld is because of issues related to transfer of power. One widely reported reason is that the Democratic Governor of Kentucky Andy Beshear would name the Senator's successor in the case of his death. This is patently **false**. The Kentucky General Assembly passed House Bill 622 in 2024, which completely stripped Democratic Governor Andy Beshear of his power to appoint a temporary Senator.

The date of August 1st is also frequently mentioned as some sort of magical date that would remove the threat of a Democratic replacement. However, HB622 has neutered these considerations completely.

The actual process is this:
- If a Senate seat becomes vacant due to death, resignation, or expulsion, the governor is legally required to call a special election. The people of Kentucky vote to fill the seat, and the winner serves out the remainder of the term.
- The Timeline: By law, a special election to fill a U.S. Senate vacancy must take place not less than 80 days and not more than 150 days after the vacancy

The full text of [HB 622](https://apps.legislature.ky.gov/record/26rs/hb622.html) is available for the studious among you. I will bet a month's supply of donuts that link remains forever unclicked.
