## What Happened on June 14

On the morning of June 14, 2026 — roughly two weeks ago — emergency medical services were dispatched to Senator Mitch McConnell's Washington, D.C., residence after a caller reported an unconscious person in need of advanced life support. The call came in at approximately 8:36 a.m., according to [Punchbowl News](https://punchbowl.news/) and dispatch recordings reviewed by [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/mcconnell-home-ems-call-unconscious-cardiac-arrest-hospitalized/).

During the dispatch audio, a medic can be heard reporting **CPR in progress** for what was described as a **cardiac arrest**. The dispatcher also referenced an **unconscious** patient at the Senator's address. McConnell's name does not appear on the recording itself, but the address matches his known residence and the timing aligns with his office's confirmation that same day that he had been hospitalized.

McConnell's office issued a brief statement on June 14: *"Senator McConnell was admitted to the hospital this morning. He is receiving excellent care."* No diagnosis or details about the nature of the emergency were provided.

On June 22, spokesperson Stephanie Penn said McConnell was *"still working closely with staff on Senate business and Kentucky matters as he continues his recovery"* but would not be voting that week. When [NBC News](https://www.nbcboston.com/news/national-international/paramedics-cardiac-arrest-mitch-mcconnell-home/3974815/) and other outlets asked about the dispatch audio on July 1, his office declined to answer questions about his condition, whether he remains hospitalized, or the authenticity of the recording — referring reporters back to the June 22 statement.

## How This Connects to the 911 Call Story

The dispatch audio that surfaced in early July adds detail to what we already knew from the [911 call transcript](/blog/911-call/) released days earlier. Together, the two records paint a picture of a serious medical emergency at the Senator's home — but his team has maintained strict silence on specifics.

Senate Majority Leader John Thune said on June 15 that McConnell was *"clearly dialed in to what's going on"* and following Senate business. McConnell, 84, has faced several health scares in recent years, including a hospitalization for flu-like symptoms earlier in 2026 and a concussion after a fall in 2023.
